State of Hawaii

Capital: Honolulu

Largest city: Honolulu

Official languages: English, Hawaiian

HI is the official United States Postal Service abbreviation for the state of Hawaii. Hawaii is a state in the Western United States, located in the Pacific Ocean about 2,000 miles from the U.S. mainland. It is the only U.S. state outside North America, the only state that is an archipelago, and the only state in the tropics.

History

Hawaii was an independent kingdom until 1893 when the monarchy was overthrown by American and European businessmen. It became a U.S. territory in 1898 and achieved statehood on August 21, 1959.

Geography

The state encompasses nearly the entire Hawaiian archipelago, 137 islands spread over 1,500 miles. The main islands are Niʻihau, Kauaʻi, Oʻahu, Molokaʻi, Lānaʻi, Kahoʻolawe, Maui, and Hawaiʻi (also known as "The Big Island").

Culture

Hawaii's culture is strongly influenced by Native Hawaiian traditions, as well as other Asian and European cultures. It is manifested in cuisine, language, music, art, theater, dance, and more.
